# Playtest notes — 2026-07-20 (first external playtester)

Source: live session on `game.dev.snowskeleton.net` with Miguel, two players, ~90 min.
This is the raw feedback sorted into actionable buckets. Nothing here is committed to the
roadmap yet except where noted; the **Open questions** section is what needs a decision.

Overall read: the interface and controls read clearly with no explanation, the co-op
"oh hey that's you" moment landed, and the failures were all systems-level (multiplayer
room transitions, missing affordances) rather than "this game isn't fun."

---

## 1. Bugs — fix these

Ordered roughly by how badly they break a session.

> a bug (D5 makes it intentional). **B13 did not reproduce** — see the note under
> the table. Regression cover for the structural ones is
> `npx ts-node server/src/verify-barriers.ts`.

### What each fix was

- **B1** — barriers are now genuinely one-way (G1), via a `Layer.BARRIER_EXIT`
  collision category only a *committed* player's mask carries. Latecomers walk in;
  nobody walks out. Covered by `verify-barriers.ts`.
- **B2** — two causes. The four `playerSpawns` were the **same point**, so a late
  joiner was created as a coincident matter body, and the solver resolves a
  zero-distance overlap by flinging one an arbitrary distance (through geometry —
  hence "in a wall", and hence off-camera, hence "invisible"). Spawns are now a
  fixed, walkable, rng-free ring. Separately, a destroyed `RemotePlayer` could keep
  receiving Colyseus `onChange` callbacks and throw inside the sync handler; views
  now carry `isDestroyed`.
- **B3** — Escape peels one layer at a time (offer picker → pause menu → gameplay)
  and the quit path is behind a confirm. Superseded later by D7's menu rebuild.
- **B4** — the **start room** was in the ordinary room-type roll but never gets
  enemies, so a `wave`/`timed` roll there produced an objective that could never
  complete, and the banner stuck forever. It's demoted to `combat`. The roll still
  happens, and combat/wave/timed carve identically, so **all 400 seeds checked keep
  byte-identical tile maps**.
- **B5** — doorway tiles are walkable, so the tile test couldn't see a shut door.
  Projectiles now consult `physics.barrierAt()`.
- **B6** — enemies are confined to their home room (same mechanism as B14).
- **B7** — bosses get a 900ms stun-immunity window after each stagger. They still
  get *shoved* during it; they just can't be chain-stunned into a statue.
- **B8** — the chest had no collider at all. It has one now
  (`PhysicsWorld.addSolidProp`).
- **B10** — two real defects: held ranged weapons rendered at 24px while every hand
  weapon rendered at 16px from equally tight art, and `holdWeaponIconAtRest` never
  mirrored, so a left-facing axe was cocked the wrong way. *The oversized **hammer**
  did not reproduce* — a war-hammer renders at exactly the same 16px as every
  sword; worth a second look in-game.
- **B11** — `makeSheetEnemyDef` defaults an enemy's death clip to **its walk frames
  reversed**, which for most creatures is indistinguishable from standing still.
  Every enemy now gets a universal flash → fade → drift flourish regardless of what
  clip its def has.
- **B12** — real and systemic. Knockback required `force - resistance > 0`, and
  `GooGold` resists **8**, so daggers (4), rapiers (5), swords (7) *and* spears (8)
  moved it not at all. A hit that falls short now still nudges
  (`KNOCKBACK_MIN_FRACTION`) but never stuns.
- **B13 — could not reproduce.** Cover blocks are plain `TILE.WALL`, one 32×32
  collider per tile, and the wall art fills all 1024px of its cell — so the collider
  is exactly the art, not larger. The snagging is more likely the solver catching on
  the internal seams between adjacent merged wall rects. **Needs a live repro before
  anything is changed.**

## 2. Gameplay changes — small, clear wins

These aren't bugs; they're friction the tester hit that we can fix without a design pivot.

- **G1 — Barriers become one-way.** Latecomers can walk *in*, nobody can walk *out*. This is
  the tester's fix for B1 and it's better than the alternatives (teleport-everyone is
  jarring; a doorway "holding zone" blocks the split-the-party play we now want to
  support). Rule: *players are committed to a room once they enter, but not everyone has
  to be inside for it to start.*
- **G2 — Grace period on room entry.** Enemies hold still for a beat when a player first
  enters, so standing-next-to-the-door enemies can't free-hit you. Pairs with B6.
- **G3 — Telegraph incoming waves.** A short banner/countdown before each wave spawns
  instead of them just appearing.
- **G4 — Slower enemies, more of them.** Both of us landed on this independently. Reduce
  per-enemy speed, raise counts, so encounters read as positioning rather than reflex.
- **G5 — Interaction prompts.** Chests give no hint that `F` opens them, or even that
  they're interactable rather than scenery. Need a generic "press F" prompt on proximity
  for chests, shop pedestals, offer pedestals, and stairs.
- **G6 — Rebind weapon switch off `Q`/`E`.** Neither of us likes it.
- **G7 — Boss rewards are first-come-first-served.** The other player took the reward.
  Fix: **each player gets their own 1-of-3 pick from the boss, and the picks are mutually
  exclusive** across the party. Scales naturally to a 4-player cap.
- **G8 — Let players exit the offer picker without choosing.** Currently you're forced to
  take something to look at anything.
- **G9 — Minimap.** Called out emphatically and unprompted. Especially load-bearing once
  players are meant to split up to find the exit.
- **G10 — Stairs / floor transition needs to read as stairs.** The tester didn't register
  the glowing tile as "this is the way down" — didn't know there *were* multiple floors.
- **G11 — Everything looks the same at zoom.** Rooms need enough texture variation to be
  distinguishable; this is the real reason zooming out felt better (see Q3).
- **G12 — Remove or hide the respawn.** It's a debug affordance; the design is permadeath.
  Keep it behind the debug menu.

## 3. Theory — bigger direction, needs design work

### 3a. Multiplayer shape
- Splitting the party to clear a floor faster is a play pattern we hadn't planned for and
  both of us now like. It creates a real tension: **stick together for power, split up for
  speed.** G1 (one-way barriers) and G9 (minimap) are its prerequisites.
- Scaling with player count: options discussed were more enemy HP, or **splitting damage
  across players (e.g. 60% each at two players)** so more bodies is still an advantage but
  not a landslide, and sticking together is incentivized. Unresolved — see Q1.
- 4-player cap seems right; beyond that the rooms get crowded.

### 3b. Economy
- **Currency drops from enemies** rather than the current HP-cost shop. The argument that
  won: currency creates *choices* — skip an early weapon to save for a better one, and
  full-clearing a floor pays better than rushing it, which is a natural counterweight to
  the speed incentive above. This effectively replaces the HP-cost mechanic.

### 3c. Movement abilities (strong idea, lots of work)
Give each class (or each weapon type) a distinct traversal move, and build floor obstacles
that each one answers differently:

| Move | Crosses |
|---|---|
| Blink | *through* thin obstacles — grates, bars — and over gaps |
| Jump | over gaps |
| Charge | *through* breakable walls |
| Dash | plain distance / i-frames |

Never through locked barriers. This turns obstacles into soft gating: a chest behind bars
is Blink-only, a chest across a gap is Jump/Blink, a crumbly wall is Charge. Rewards
exploration and gives classes an identity beyond stats.

### 3d. Puzzles (revives the deferred Floor-4 puzzle rooms)
The tester's framing — *small, non-essential, multiple solutions* — is a much better scope
than what got deferred. Concrete ideas:
- Pressure plates: a second player stands on it, **or** you shove a boulder onto it.
- Wooden barriers you burn with magic; stone you shatter with a hammer; crumbly walls you
  blow up or Charge through.
- Torches: light them all to reveal a chest — or the inverse, extinguish them.
- A pot resting on a plate: moving/breaking it triggers a trap *or* reveals a secret.
- Four-Swords-style co-op: boulders that need two players, oversized plates needing
  multiple bodies or objects.

Because they're non-essential, none of them can softlock a party — which was the exact
concern that deferred puzzles in the first place.

### 3e. Weapon abilities
Per-weapon special attacks beyond the current stab/smash: AOE blast, buckshot spread,
single heavy shot. Possibly "different weapons cast different spells" rather than spells
being a Mage-only pool. The nova is already built and sitting unused.

### 3f. Classes vs. universal weapons
Open. Either (a) restrict weapon categories to classes, each starting with a basic weapon
of its type and finding the rest in-run, or (b) drop classes and let anyone use anything.
Caveat: weapon categories are unevenly populated right now. See Q2.

### 3g. Endgame and replay
- Endless scaling (Risk of Rain 2) was flagged as a failure mode — it becomes unplayable
  rather than climactic. Prefer a **defined ending with meta-unlocks** (Hades / Isaac):
  unlock weapons, classes, items, bosses across runs; track per-character completion.
- Multiple themed **dungeons**, each unlocked by clearing the last. Gated hard by art
  budget — a new dungeon means a new enemy set.
- Art is the real constraint on all of 3g. Plan of record: ship a v1 with one level set,
  and only justify a second set if people like it.

## 4. Deferred by design (raised and deliberately not doing)

- **Respawn on death** — the game is permadeath; the existing respawn is a test tool.
- **Failure state on timed rooms** — miss the timer, lose the chest, keep your life.
- **Teleporting the whole party into a room when one player enters** — jarring, and it
  kills the split-the-party option. One-way barriers (G1) instead.

---

## 5. Not-yet-started, confirmed still wanted

- **Sound.** Zero audio; the tester noticed within three minutes. Effort™, but it's the
  single biggest gap between this and something that feels like a game.
- **Controller support.** Gamepad input exists but is untested; m/kb is the default only
  because it's easiest to test, and it "doesn't feel great."
- **Weapon balance pass.** All weapons are visually final; none are balanced against each
  other.

## 6. Decisions taken (2026-07-21)

These are settled. Everything above that contradicts them is superseded.

- **D1 — Multiplayer scaling is enemy HP, not player damage.** +50% enemy max HP per
  additional player: ×1.0 / ×1.5 / ×2.0 / ×2.5 for 1–4 players. Damage-split is rejected —
  nerfing the player for bringing a friend feels bad. Party cap stays 4.
- **D2 — Classes stay, and become the game's identity axis.** Weapon categories are
  restricted per class; each class gets its own movement ability (3c) *and* a distinct
  special power. First one designed: **Knight — a regenerating shield**, so the melee class
  can hold the front line without paying HP for it. Rogue/Ranger/Mage powers are TBD.
- **D3 — Camera zoom is locked at the default.** Remove the zoom option from
  `client/src/options/gameOptions.ts`. The zoomed-out view is a symptom of G11 (everything
  looks the same); fix the textures, don't hand players a workaround that costs immersion.
- **D4 — Currency replaces the HP-cost shop.** Enemies drop gold. Shop items come in three
  tiers: **50g / 100g / 150g**. Target economy: a player who clears ~70% of a floor can
  afford roughly **one 100g item per floor**. Because the drop rate depends on enemy count
  and room count — both of which will keep moving — this must be built as a *tunable
  budget*, not scattered literals. See Q8.
- **D5 — Descending without clearing is intentional.** Skipping loot for speed (and
  staying weaker) is exactly the kind of choice we want available. In multiplayer, a player
  on the stairs raises a prompt — *"Player X wants to descend. Join them to continue"* — and
  the party only descends once **every** player is on the stairs.
- **D6 — Movement abilities are class-bound** (not weapon-bound).
- **D7 — Rebuild the menu system.** A real pause menu you can *resume* from. Escape opens
  it; quitting the run is an explicit, confirmed choice inside it. (Supersedes B3 — that
  bug gets fixed by this work rather than patched separately.)
- **D8 — Matchmaking / lobby.** At game start the player chooses **Solo** or **Shared**.
  Shared shows a browsable list of open rooms with the option to create a new one. ("Solo"
  still runs on the server; a genuinely-offline build is a possible later shipping mode.)

### Decisions taken, round 2

- **D9 — Class weapon restrictions are a small exclusive list, not a full partition.** Most
  weapons stay open to everyone; each class owns a few categories outright, so classes feel
  distinct without collapsing the loot pool.

  | Class | Exclusive categories | Count |
  |---|---|---|
  | Knight | hammers, maces | 6 |
  | Mage | staves | 6 |
  | Ranger | bows, crossbows | 3 |
  | Rogue | daggers, thrown | 6 |
  | *(open to all)* | swords, axes, spears, rapiers | 31 |

  Crossbows go to Ranger so its exclusive pool isn't two bows. Swords are deliberately the
  shared backbone (18 of them).

- **D10 — Loot rolls are filtered by party composition.** A weapon nobody present can use
  never rolls — no staves without a Mage. Applies to shops, chests, boss/shrine offers.
  This is a filter on the roll, not a post-hoc reject, so the odds stay correct.
- **D11 — The movement ability has its own cooldown**, separate from the class special
  power and from weapon cooldowns.
- **D12 — Rooms are public-listed or private-by-code**, player's choice at creation.
  **Lobby-only joins** — you cannot drop into a run in progress.
- **D13 — Enemy HP scaling is fixed at run start** from the party size; it does not rescale
  when players join or drop.
- **D14 — Gold and free offer pedestals coexist.** Boss/chest offers stay free picks.
  **Shrines cost gold** — that's gold's second sink. Gold may also appear as one of the
  choices on an offer pedestal.
- **D15 — Only the Knight's special power is designed for now** (regenerating shield).
  Build it as the pattern; Rogue/Ranger/Mage powers get designed once we've seen it work.

- **D17 — Gold is one shared party purse.** Chosen deliberately *because* it creates
  conflict: a contested resource makes players negotiate, and that builds a better co-op
  session than four isolated wallets. (Note this cuts against G7's reasoning for boss
  rewards — that's fine. Rewards are personal, money is communal.)
- **D18 — Weapon restriction is one-way: weapons are restricted to classes, classes are not
  restricted to weapons.** A staff is Mage-only; the Mage may still swing a broadsword.
  Reversible later if classes read too weakly.

  **Implementation constraint (non-negotiable):** the restriction is **type-based, not
  string-based**. Each class declares the weapon *categories* it can use as a compiler-checked
  list on its `CharacterConfig` — not an id→class lookup table, not a string tag on the
  weapon. A weapon not in the class's list cannot be equipped, and the check is one
  membership test the compiler can verify covers every category. Adding a category without
  assigning it is a build error, not a silent "nobody can use this."

### D16 — The currency model

The economy is **budgeted, not per-enemy-priced.** Per-enemy gold values look simpler but
re-break the target every time we add an enemy or change room counts — which we will keep
doing. Instead:

- The anchor constants are the *design intent*, stated directly:
  `SHOP_TIER_COSTS = { low: 50, mid: 100, high: 150 }`,
  `TARGET_SPEND_PER_PLAYER_PER_FLOOR = 100` (one mid item),
  `TARGET_CLEAR_FRACTION = 0.7`.
- The floor's total gold is *derived*: `budget = TARGET_SPEND / TARGET_CLEAR_FRACTION`
  (≈143g) `× playerCount`. Clear everything and you're ~43g toward the next thing; clear
  70% and you afford exactly one mid item. Rush the floor and the shortfall *is* the cost
  of rushing — which is precisely the interesting choice D5 is protecting.
- `SpawnDirector` divides that budget across what it just spawned, weighted by a
  **`goldWeight` getter on each `Enemy` subclass** (default 1; a tanky enemy overrides it
  higher). Same OO shape as every other enemy stat — no id→gold table. The boss takes a
  fixed share off the top before the rabble split. Drops round to 5g so they read as coins.
- **Tier costs stay fixed and the per-floor budget stays roughly flat across floors.** What
  scales with depth is the *quality* of what's in the shop, not the price tag. This keeps
  "about one item per floor" true on floor 9 as well as floor 1, and keeps prices something
  a player can learn.

Knobs this exposes, in the order you'd reach for them: the three tier costs → the target
spend/clear-fraction pair → `goldWeight` on a specific enemy → boss/chest share → the
shrine cost.

## 7. Still open — next round of questions

8. **Is gold shared or per-player?** One party purse (simple, but invites the same
   first-come grab that G7 is fixing) or an individual wallet each (matches the per-player
   boss pick, but needs a per-player split rule on every drop).
9. **Are the exclusive categories "only I can use" or also "only I can use *this kind*"?**
   D9 says a Mage owns staves — but may a Mage also swing a broadsword? Letting them makes
   staves feel like a bonus rather than an identity; forbidding heavy weapons to the Mage
   (a second, negative axis) makes the classes read much more strongly. Same question for
   whether the Knight can carry a dagger.
10. **What does a class start with?** Presumably its exclusive category's basic weapon
    (Knight → club, Mage → oak staff, Ranger → shortbow, Rogue → curved dagger). Confirm,
    and confirm the current `CHARACTER_REGISTRY.defaultWeaponId` values should change to
    match.
11. **Solo party-filter degenerate case.** A solo Mage filters loot to staves + the shared
    pool (37 of 42 weapons). A solo Ranger filters to bows/crossbows + shared (34). Fine.
    But do we want a floor of pure shared-pool swords to feel repetitive — i.e. should the
    roll weight *favor* the party's exclusive categories rather than merely permit them?
12. **Where does the Knight's shield live architecturally?** It's the first thing that is a
    class ability rather than a weapon spell or an upgrade. Is `ClassPower` a new OO family
    alongside `Upgrade`/`Spell`, or is it just a `Spell` the class always has? (My read: a
    passive regen shield isn't a `Spell` at all — it's closer to an always-on `Upgrade` the
    class is born with. Worth deciding before the first line of code.)
13. **How much of the lobby (D8/D12) is real matchmaking vs. UI?** Colyseus already does
    room listing and join-by-id; the work is mostly a lobby scene plus a `private` flag.
    Do you want persistent room names/codes, or is a generated code fine?

## 8. Suggested ordering

1. **Session-enders first:** B1 (barrier lockout) → B2 (`P`-join into a wall) → D7's menu
   rebuild, which absorbs B3.
2. **Cheap feel wins:** G5 (interaction prompts), G2 (entry grace), G4 (slower/more
   enemies), G12, D3 (lock zoom), B10/B11 (weapon scale, death feedback).
3. **"Co-op actually works" milestone:** G1 (one-way barriers), G7 (per-player boss
   rewards), D5 (party stairs prompt), G9 (minimap), D1 (HP scaling).
4. **New systems, in dependency order:** D4 currency → D8 lobby → D2/D6 class powers and
   movement abilities → 3d puzzles (which depend on the movement abilities existing).
